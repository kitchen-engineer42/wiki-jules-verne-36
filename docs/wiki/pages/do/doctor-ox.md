# Doctor Ox

## Overview

The narrative frames him as an enigma, asking "Who, then, was this personage, known by the singular name of Doctor Ox?" (DOSE-004-002).

He is no charlatan but "a bold savant, a physiologist, whose works were known and highly estimated throughout learned Europe" (DOSE-004-003).

He works from "Doctor Ox's laboratory, which was situated outside the town, near the Oudenarde gate" (DOSE-005-009).

## Role in the Story

He offers to light the long-benighted town for nothing, his public excuse being "that Doctor Ox bears the whole expense of his experiment" (DOSE-003-039).

His proposal is to use "a more modern and twenty-fold more brilliant gas, oxyhydric gas, produced by mixing hydrogen and oxygen" (DOSE-004-009).

But the lighting is only a mask, for he means "under this pretext, design to make some great physiological experiment" (DOSE-004-007).

His true method is laid bare at the end: "After having laid down his gas-pipes, he had saturated, first the public buildings, then the private dwellings, finally the streets of Quiquendone, with pure oxygen, without letting in the least atom of hydrogen" (DOSE-017-002).

His experiment is cut short when "the gasworks had just blown up" (DOSE-015-006).

So it is that "a providential explosion put an end to this dangerous experiment, and abolished Doctor Ox's gas-works" (DOSE-017-006).

## Character & Traits

He is "an able chemist as well as an ingenious physiologist" (DOSE-004-010), master of the science he abuses.

His detachment is chilling: warned that the townsfolk may suffer, he answers "So much the worse for them!" (DOSE-004-019).

His ambition is boundless, for he declares triumphantly that "we shall reform the world!" (DOSE-004-031).

As the placid town ignites into quarrels he can barely hide his glee, "with difficulty repressed a sigh of satisfaction" (DOSE-005-050).

## Relationships

- Gédéon Ygène is his assistant and collaborator, for "Doctor Ox had arrived at Quiquendone five months before, accompanied by his assistant, who answered to the name of Gédéon Ygène" (DOSE-004-006).

- [[Van Tricasse]] — the phlegmatic burgomaster on whose torpid town he experiments, and whom, as the oxygen takes hold, he studies: "As for Doctor Ox, he looked at the burgomaster with sly attention" (DOSE-005-030).

## Appearances

- [[Doctor Ox's Experiment]] — at whose close the narrator recoils from the physiologist's conclusion: "Such is Doctor Ox's theory; but we are not bound to accept it, and for ourselves we utterly reject it" (DOSE-017-008).
