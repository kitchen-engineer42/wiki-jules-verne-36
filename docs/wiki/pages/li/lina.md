# Lina

## Overview

She is dearer than a mere servant — "a pretty mulatto, Miss Lina, who is more of a companion than a servant" (EHLA-013-057) to her mistress.

Where Minha grieves to leave home, "the joyous Lina was quite unaffected at leaving Iquitos" (EHLA-006-037).

## Role in the Story

Her playful curiosity saves a life, for Fragoso thanks her for "the good idea she had had of following the liana" (EHLA-007-145), and indeed "Fragoso was very grateful to the mulatto for having taken the initiative in his rescue" (EHLA-008-020).

Later her sharp eye guards the family, since "Fragoso, urged on by Lina, did not cease to watch Torres" (EHLA-016-091). Through it all "Lina, who was to remain in the service of Minha" (EHLA-018-042) keeps her place beside her mistress.

## Character & Traits

She is irrepressibly gay — "The noisy laughter of Lina was alone sufficiently shrill to ring out with its joyous note" (EHLA-007-040) — and "Lina kept it going with her good humor" (EHLA-018-046).

Her love is selfless, for "she was more grateful to the brave Fragoso than if it was herself that he had saved" (EHLA-018-003).

## Relationships

- [[Fragoso]] — the barber she rescued and weds, so that "the charming Lina was engaged to Fragoso" (EHLA-018-008); he insists at the last "it is not to me that all this happiness is due, it is due to Lina" (EHLA-039-035).

- [[Minha Garral]] — her mistress and near-sister, whom she never leaves; in Joam's darkest hour "Minha and Lina kept near her, waiting for the time when she would be admitted to see her husband" (EHLA-026-002).

- [[Yaquita]] — the mother of the house, whom she serves, for "Yaquita, aided by Lina and Cybele, was getting everything in order" (EHLA-010-014).

## Appearances

- [[Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]] — at the double wedding "then came Fragoso, holding the hand of Lina" (EHLA-040-039), and afterward "Fragoso and Lina were praised and petted by all the family" (EHLA-039-038).

- On the family's excursions her spirits lead the way: "let us look around, my dear mistress, for it is very fine" (EHLA-016-047).
