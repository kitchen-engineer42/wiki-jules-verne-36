# Death of Captain Nemo

## Overview

The death of Captain Nemo closes the story of Lincoln Island's mysterious benefactor—dying aboard the *Nautilus* in his submarine grotto, the recluse reveals himself to the colonists and, at his own wish, is entombed with his vessel beneath the sea, an event chronicled in *[[The Mysterious Island]]*.

He wished to die aboard his ship. It was useless to think of moving him to Granite House, for he had expressed his wish to remain on the *Nautilus* to the last (SI-017-003).

## What Happens

He asked one final favour. "I shall die here—it is my wish," he said, "and therefore I have a request to make of you" (SI-017-012).

He bound the colonists by a promise. "Promise, then," continued Captain Nemo, "to carry out my last wishes, and I shall be repaid for all I have done" (SI-017-021).

Life ebbed without pain. Captain Nemo suffered no pain but was visibly sinking, his noble features, paled by the approach of death, perfectly calm (SI-017-076).

He composed himself for the end. Shortly after midnight, by a supreme effort, Captain Nemo succeeded in folding his arms across his breast, as if to die in that attitude (SI-017-078).

## Significance

His last words named his cause. Then, murmuring the words "God and my country!", [[Captain Nemo]] quietly expired (SI-017-079).

The recluse was unmasked in death. Cyrus Harding, bending low, closed the eyes of him who had once been the Prince Dakkar, now no more than a name (SI-017-080).

The ship became his tomb. The stop-cocks were opened, the reservoirs filled, and the *Nautilus*, slowly sinking, disappeared beneath the waters as the tomb of Captain Nemo (SI-017-089).

## Participants & Setting

- The scene unfolds in the *Nautilus*, moored in the dark cavern of Dakkar Grotto beneath Lincoln Island, its saloon still flooded with electric light.
- Cyrus Harding, Gideon Spilett, Pencroft, Herbert, Neb, and Ayrton keep vigil at the deathbed of the man they knew only as the island's unseen protector.
- The dying man is revealed as Prince Dakkar, builder of the *Nautilus*; his last wish is that the colonists sink the vessel over him, sealing both recluse and ship beneath the sea.
