# Torres

## Overview

He is introduced with the words "Torres--for that was his name--unlike the majority of his companions, was neither half-breed, Indian, nor negro" (EHLA-001-007).

His true calling is a disreputable one, for the jangada gives refuge, unknowingly, to "an old captain of the woods" (EHLA-014-002).

He is a rootless adventurer who, "after having suddenly abandoned the trade of the slave hunter, which he carried on in the province of Para, Torres had ascended the basin of the Amazon" (EHLA-001-019).

## Role in the Story

From the first he means to trade a secret document for a fortune, gloating over his intended victim: "He will pay, if I wish it, with all his fortune, as he ought to pay with all his blood!" (EHLA-001-018).

Aboard the raft he at last confronts the fazender with his hidden identity, telling him he is "who, twenty-five years ago, were a clerk in the governor-general's office at Tijuco, and you are the man who was sentenced to death in this affair of the robbery and murder!" (EHLA-020-005).

He names his price for the exonerating paper, warning that "and you will not have it until your daughter has become my wife" (EHLA-020-048).

Refused, he carries out his threat — the record says he "threatened to denounce Joam Garral as being Joam Dacosta, if he declined to purchase his silence" (EHLA-022-034).

His end comes in a duel with the son he has wronged: "A second thrust of the manchetta pierced his heart" (EHLA-026-105).

The proof he hoarded is found on his corpse, for "Torres had declined to part with it until after his marriage with Dacosta's daughter--that is to say, when it would have been impossible to undo an accomplished fact" (EHLA-034-006).

## Character & Traits

His avarice shows at once as, "scrutinizing the paper with greedy eyes" (EHLA-001-014), he reckons its worth in money.

Reading the secret cipher, "he smiled--and a most unpleasant smile it was" (EHLA-001-012).

His face betrays him to a careful eye: "There was an entire want of frankness in the eyes, whose look changed unceasingly, as if he was afraid to fix them anywhere" (EHLA-013-081).

The narrative brands his purpose plainly: "The scoundrel was on his way to the fazenda with the idea of consummating a vile scheme of extortion" (EHLA-022-021).

His greed ruins even his own scheme, through "his desire to wait till the very last moment, so as to get the very best price for the document he possessed" (EHLA-026-087).

## Relationships

- [[Joam Garral]] — the fazender whose secret he holds; he will not be bought off with money alone, declaring "I wish to be the son-in-law of Joam Garral, and I will" (EHLA-020-025).

- Benito, Joam's son, is the man who kills him; the young man returns to his father crying "Torres is dead, father; killed by my hand!" (EHLA-026-122).

- Minha, whose hand he covets as the price of the document, feels for him "an instinctive repulsion which she was at no pains to conceal" (EHLA-015-030).

## Appearances

- [[Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]] — in which, killed before he can sell his secret, "Torres died without accomplishing his work, and carried his secret with him" (EHLA-039-026).
