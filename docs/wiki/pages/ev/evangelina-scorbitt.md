# Evangelina Scorbitt

## Overview

She is a wealthy Baltimore widow: "Mrs. Evangelina Scorbitt had four millions of good sound dollars, or twenty millions of francs, which came to her from John P. Scorbitt" (TT-004-010).

She is known throughout for "the love and esteem felt towards the rich capitalist widow of New Park" (TT-012-006).

## Role in the Story

From the first she offers her purse: "I will be the woman in the case and bring to it my pecuniary assistance" (TT-001-015).

When the North Pole is bought at auction, "it was Mrs. Evangelina Scorbitt who had put the necessary amount into this affair" (TT-004-006).

For Maston's sake she funds it blindly, having "gladly agreed to put a few hundred thousand dollars in the affair of the N.P.P.A. without having the least idea of what they intended to do with it" (TT-004-011).

When the schemers are hunted she saves them too: "Mrs. Evangelina Scorbitt had come to his help with a heavy purse of gold, and he had made his escape" (TT-016-007).

## Character & Traits

No mathematician herself, "Mrs. Evangelina Scorbitt, although the least figuring gave her a headache, had yet a great liking for mathematicians" (TT-004-008).

She takes his part in all things, and "was not slow to share his indignation" (TT-005-016).

Her loyalty knows no fear: "And they can kill me with you," said Mrs. Evangelina Scorbitt (TT-012-028).

She trembles for him always, so that "Mrs. Evangelina Scorbitt trembled lest he would become the victim of a universal cry for vengeance" (TT-013-006).

Even after the great fiasco "Mrs. Evangelina Scorbitt she could not be angry with him, most excellent lady" (TT-020-042).

## Relationships

- [[J. T. Maston]] — the Gun Club calculator she adores; "Mrs. Evangelina Scorbitt, jealous of his fame, felt for him an admiration which ended in love" (TT-006-004), and in the end "the Secretary of the Gun Club made Mrs. Evangelina Scorbitt Mrs. J. T. Maston" (TT-020-054).

- [[Impey Barbicane]] — the Gun Club's president, who hails her as "the generous patron of our Society" (TT-012-022).

- Her devotion even preserves Maston, for "this great man owed his life to the devotion of a loving woman" (TT-016-008).

## Appearances

- [[Topsy-Turvy]] — at whose failure she stands by him, "Absolutely crushed and heart-broken, she had followed him" (TT-020-050).
