# Ole Kamp

## Overview

He is a foster-son of the Hansen household, for "Ole Kamp, a son of his wife's sister, had also been brought up like one of his own children" (TN-003-004).

A young Norwegian seaman, "he made regular voyages upon large fishing vessels, and rose to the rank of mate when he was but twenty-one" (TN-004-005).

Against all hope he proves one "who, by a miracle, had survived the shipwreck" (TN-020-001) of his vessel.

## Role in the Story

His letters home hint at a secret fortune, for "Hulda was considerably surprised at the persistency with which Ole alluded in his letters to the fortune that was to be his on his return" (TN-005-001).

When his ship founders "he inclosed the ticket in a bottle and threw it into the sea with the last farewell for Hulda" (TN-012-001).

His bottle-message reads: "The 'Viking' is going down. I have only this ticket left of all I hoped to bring back to you" (TN-011-116).

Yet he is not lost: "Ole Kamp and four of his comrades succeeded in leaping upon the iceberg, just as the vessel went down" (TN-020-012).

At the climax it is learned that he "had returned, and that he had won the grand prize of the Schools Lottery" (TN-020-003).

## Character & Traits

His devotion is whole-hearted: "I am firmly resolved to make my Hulda the happiest woman in the country" (TN-004-043).

He means this to be his last voyage, vowing "I shall not follow the sea any more after my marriage" (TN-004-033).

His courage saves him, for he "retained all his presence of mind at the time of the shipwreck" (TN-012-013).

Others reckon him "a brave, honest fellow, of Joel's stamp" (TN-011-031).

## Relationships

- [[Hulda Hansen]] — his betrothed and foster-cousin, to whom he returns at the last, crying "Yes, it is I--your betrothed--soon to be your husband!" (TN-020-005).

- His last thought as the ship went down was for her, when "Ole Kamp, as the ship went down, bequeathed to Hulda the sole earthly possession left him" (TN-015-032).

- Sylvius Hog, the benefactor Hulda and Joel had rescued, champions his cause out of gratitude for "the invaluable service rendered him by the young man's betrothed" (TN-011-062).

## Appearances

- [[Ticket No. 9672]] — at whose climactic drawing the sailor mourned as drowned reappears alive: "It was Ole Kamp!" (TN-019-073).
