# Benito Garral

## Overview

He first appears identifying his family, saying "My father, Joam Garral, has his farm about three miles from here" (EHLA-002-079).

Now "aged twenty-one, and quick, brave, and sympathetic" (EHLA-003-040), he contrasts with his graver friend Manoel.

He is a keen sportsman, for "Benito, as was his wont, strolled off with his gun into the adjoining woods" (EHLA-012-046).

## Role in the Story

When the police come to arrest his father, he "placed himself between Joam Garral and Torres" (EHLA-020-077).

Once he resolves to hunt down the blackmailer, "Benito's resolution admitted of no discussion" (EHLA-026-015).

He kills Torres in their duel and cries "I have killed him!" (EHLA-027-009).

But the exonerating document has sunk with the body, and "Benito was quite overwhelmed, and accused himself of having destroyed his father" (EHLA-027-001).

He throws himself into the river-search, resolving to "search with my eyes--with my hands" (EHLA-029-011).

His search at last succeeds, and he holds up the recovered case: "that is the document which proves my father's innocence!" (EHLA-031-058).

## Character & Traits

He is "possessed of a cheerful disposition, an active mind, a lively intelligence" (EHLA-003-031).

On the hunt "Benito showed more than virtue, and even genuine heroism" (EHLA-007-055).

His filial fury burns so hot that Manoel tries in vain to calm "Benito, whose head seemed on fire" (EHLA-026-021).

Yet in the duel itself "his coolness was unruffled, much more so than that of Torres" (EHLA-026-097).

Through the exhausting search "Benito was resolved to go as far as his mental powers and physical energies would let him" (EHLA-030-031).

## Relationships

- [[Joam Garral]] — his father, whose innocence he never doubts and whom he sets out to avenge: "it is for me alone to avenge my father" (EHLA-026-090).

- [[Torres]] — the blackmailer he confronts and kills, returning to tell his father "Torres is dead, father; killed by my hand!" (EHLA-026-122).

- Minha, his sister, shares his devoted upbringing, for "Benito and Minha, the grandchildren of the old Portuguese, became worthy of their grandfather, children worthy of Joam and Yaquita" (EHLA-003-029).

## Appearances

- [[Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]] — the chronicle of the family's raft voyage, on which he asks the priest "do you know a more agreeable way of traveling?" (EHLA-010-016).
