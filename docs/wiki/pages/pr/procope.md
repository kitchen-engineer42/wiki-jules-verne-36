# Lieutenant Procope

## Overview

He is a young officer of the sea: "Lieutenant Procope, a man of about thirty years of age" (OC-011-001).

Count Timascheff presents him plainly — "This is Lieutenant Procope, the officer in command of the _Dobryna_" (OC-029-007) — and among the company he is reckoned "Lieutenant Procope, perhaps the greatest expert in the party" (OC-024-006).

## Role in the Story

His seamanship first reveals the catastrophe, for it is "the necessary inference that Lieutenant Procope drew from the round of the earth being completed" (OC-016-003) that something is gravely wrong, and he ventures the conjecture "that a fragment of considerable magnitude has been detached from the earth" (OC-016-006).

He is also the mind behind the escape: it is "the plan of which Lieutenant Procope had thus become the originator" (OC-042-060), and "To Lieutenant Procope himself was entrusted the superintendence of the construction of the montgolfier" (OC-042-061).

## Character & Traits

He does not lose heart — "Neither Servadac, nor the count, nor Lieutenant Procope were men to be easily disheartened" (OC-021-011) — and does his duty to the last, telling the count "I have done everything that man could do; but our case is desperate" (OC-013-030).

He is a prudent seaman: "Lieutenant Procope, following the example of many Arctic explorers, had the precaution to have the ice beveled away from the keels" (OC-024-009).

## Relationships

- [[Hector Servadac]] — the captain he serves beside and would follow anywhere, for "A strong desire was expressed by Lieutenant Procope that he should be allowed to accompany Captain Servadac" (OC-025-026).

- Count Timascheff — his employer and the Dobryna's owner, to whom "Lieutenant Procope proceeded to impart to the count his anxiety about the situation of his yacht" (OC-031-047).

- [[Isaac Hakkabut]] — the trader whose Hansa shares the anchorage, so that Procope notes "the elevation of the _Dobryna's_ and _Hansa's_ waterline" (OC-031-039).

## Appearances

- [[Off on a Comet]] — where his foresight provides for the wreck, "Lieutenant Procope took the best measures he could to insure a few days' supply of food for any who might escape ashore" (OC-013-032).

- At the climactic balloon ascent "Chronometer in hand, Lieutenant Procope stood marking the minutes and seconds as they fled" (OC-044-014), until "Lieutenant Procope observed that the comet deviated sensibly in an oblique course" (OC-044-019).
