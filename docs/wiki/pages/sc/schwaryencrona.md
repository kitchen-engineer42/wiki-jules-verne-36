# Doctor Schwaryencrona

## Overview

He is a scholar of world renown — "a scholar whose face is more universally known than that of Dr. Schwaryencrona, of Stockholm" (WC-001-002).

His fortune rests on cod-liver oil, for "the prince of this special industry is the celebrated Dr. Schwaryencrona" (WC-001-004), and "Doctor Schwaryencrona lived in a magnificent house in Stockholm" (WC-004-002).

## Role in the Story

He is a self-made man, since "Mr. Malarius was the son of the physician, and Doctor Schwaryencrona only the son of a simple fisherman" (WC-002-033).

He pursues Erik's mystery with a detective's eye — "Dr. Schwaryencrona was immediately struck by the fact that the date of his departure" (WC-009-022) matched the detective's visit — and his public inquiries flush out the guilty, as "The first advertisements of Doctor Schwaryencrona disturbed Noah Jones" (WC-022-021).

## Character & Traits

He meets alarm with composure, answering "with perfect calmness" (WC-011-012), yet he is wedded to his ideas, so that once persuaded "Doctor Schwaryencrona asked nothing more, and only saw in it the entire confirmation of his theory" (WC-004-080).

He is beloved wherever known, greeted by "the well-known and popular physiognomy of Dr. Schwaryencrona" (WC-016-015).

## Relationships

- [[Erik]] — the foundling he raises as his own; "I will willingly tell you Erik's history, for I know that I can rely upon your discretion" (WC-004-068), and he provides "Doctor Schwaryencrona judged rightly that the teachings of this school, which was one of the wonders of Stockholm" (WC-005-006) for the boy's schooling.

- Mr. Bredejord — his lifelong friend and sparring partner, who teases "My dear Schwaryencrona, you know very well you are only losing your time lecturing me" (WC-004-060).

- [[Tudor Brown]] — the sinister stranger whose interest betrays him, for "the fact of his seeking out Doctor Schwaryencrona indicated the connection" (WC-012-037).

## Appearances

- [[The Waif of the Cynthia]] — where his circle is won over: "Dr. Schwaryencrona, Mr. Bredejord and Professor Hochstedt ended by becoming interested, and finally adopted his views as their own" (WC-008-019).

- At the rescue none can "describe the unbounded joy of Dr. Schwaryencrona, Mr. Bredejord, Mr. Malarius, and Otto when they found them well and safe" (WC-020-012), and long after, "Dr. Schwaryencrona, Mr. Bredejord, and Professor Hochstedt still play innumerable games of whist" (WC-022-045).
