## Overview

Tsalal Island is the fictional Antarctic island inherited from Edgar Allan Poe's narrative of Arthur Gordon Pym, and the goal of the schooner Halbrane in An Antarctic Mystery. The permanent brightness would not fail the searchers before they should have reached Tsalal Island, where they felt no doubt of finding the men of the Jane. (AM-012-003)

## In the Narrative

The whole expedition is bounded by the island. Captain Len Guy confirms the destination flatly — "Tsalal Island, of course." (AM-009-015) Nine recruits sign articles for a campaign that was not to extend beyond Tsalal Island. (AM-009-045)

Poe's account shapes every expectation: according to Arthur Pym's narrative, there were thousands of natives on Tsalal Island, raising fears that survivors are held prisoner. (AM-009-035) As the schooner nears the ice, hope rises: "In a week our schooner might come in sight of Tsalal Island." (AM-014-003)

## Geography & Features

A remote island of the far Southern Ocean, Tsalal is imagined as warm-watered and thickly peopled in Pym's telling — the threshold beyond which Verne's continuation pushes into unmapped Antarctic waters.
