# Sylvius Hogg

## Overview

He is a man of standing: "Sylvius Hogg was a professor of law at Christiania" (TN-009-005).

His eminence is national — "Sylvius Hogg being one of the most influential men in the country" (TN-009-007), so that "the name of Sylvius Hogg was a household word throughout Norway" (TN-010-002).

## Role in the Story

Received and nursed by the Hansens after an accident, he repays them tirelessly, for "the deep interest he took in the Hansen family was well known" (TN-013-024).

To find Ole's ship he leaves no stone unturned: "he was resolved to multiply the chances of finding some trace of the missing" Viking (TN-013-060), and in the end "Sylvius Hogg had succeeded in getting the ticket out of Sandgoist's hands, having repurchased it from him" (TN-020-018).

Through Hulda's darkest weeks, "Sylvius Hogg was untiring in his efforts to console the young girl" (TN-012-046).

## Character & Traits

He is an ardent patriot — "Sylvius Hogg was a thorough Norwegian in heart and in soul, and stoutly defended her rights upon all occasions" (TN-009-011).

His generosity moves the family to cry "How kind you are, Mister Sylvius" (TN-011-024), and his motives are selfless: "It was not for the sake of an improbable chance of gain that I wished to restore it to Hulda, my brave Ole" (TN-020-019).

## Relationships

- [[Hulda Hansen]] — the young hostess he champions; at the great drawing "Sylvius Hogg had risen to his feet, and seized Hulda Hansen's hand" (TN-019-061).

- [[Ole Kamp]] — the lost betrothed whose story moves him; the tale even asks "how had Sylvius Hogg become acquainted with Ole Kamp's history" (TN-020-007).

- Joel Hansen — Hulda's brother and his companion, whom "Sylvius Hogg and Hulda always went to meet him" (TN-010-078) on his return from the heights.

## Appearances

- [[Ticket No. 9672]] — where he throws himself into the search, "Sylvius Hogg was hastening toward Bergen" (TN-013-001) to organize the hunt for the Viking.

- At the public drawing "Sylvius Hogg, and Hulda and Joel walked up the passage-way that had been cleared for them" (TN-019-016), and the tale closes with guests come "to see Sylvius Hogg, professor of law and a member of the Storthing, dance" (TN-020-026).
