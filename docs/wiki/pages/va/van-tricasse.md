# Van Tricasse

## Overview

He is the chief magistrate of a drowsy Flemish town, priding himself that "he had the honour to be the first magistrate of Quiquendone" (DOSE-008-004).

His line is among the oldest in the town, for a family portrait "represented an ancestor of the Van Tricasses, whose authentic genealogy dates back to the fourteenth century" (DOSE-002-016).

His house even regulates its marriages by custom: "From 1340 it had invariably happened that a Van Tricasse, when left a widower, had remarried a Van Tricasse younger than himself" (DOSE-002-022).

## Role in the Story

Doctor Ox's secret flood of oxygen works its first change on him, so that "the most astounded personage in the town was Van Tricasse the burgomaster" (DOSE-008-003).

Roused to belligerence, "the burgomaster himself came forward, and in a sublime harangue made short work of those pusillanimous people who disguise their fear under a veil of prudence" (DOSE-011-023).

He carries the town to the brink of war: "The burgomaster then took it upon himself to put the armies in motion, and in the name of the town he promised the honours of a triumph" (DOSE-011-028).

He convenes the council of war: "The burgomaster then assembled the dignitaries of the town" (DOSE-014-009).

Yet the war never comes, since in the end "it was because the burgomaster and the counsellor could not make up their minds to decide upon it" (DOSE-016-005).

## Character & Traits

In his natural state "the burgomaster Van Tricasse was phlegm personified" (DOSE-002-018).

He makes a virtue of never deciding, holding that a man who dies "without ever having decided upon anything during his life, has very nearly attained to perfection" (DOSE-002-012).

His days move at a glacial pace: "It was at a quarter before four that Van Tricasse lighted his enormous pipe, which could hold a quart of tobacco, and it was at thirty-five minutes past five that he finished smoking it" (DOSE-003-002).

He weighs every word, being "the burgomaster, who did not speak until after a good quarter of an hour of reflection" (DOSE-002-006).

Under the oxygen his torpor gives way to feverish energy, so that "his brain worked more during this morning than it had probably worked before for forty years" (DOSE-008-005).

## Relationships

- His placid household shelters "his wife, Madame Brigitte Van Tricasse, his daughter, Suzel Van Tricasse, and his domestic, Lotchè Janshéu" (DOSE-002-017).

- Counsellor Niklausse, his lifelong fellow-deliberator, becomes his near-antagonist under the oxygen, when "a hostile meeting was about to take place between the Burgomaster Van Tricasse and the Counsellor Niklausse" (DOSE-013-077).

## Appearances

- [[Doctor Ox's Experiment]] — at the close of which the town's torpor returns and the ancient family custom is fulfilled, for of his aged wife the chronicle records that "she died ten years later, at the proper time, and the burgomaster married Mademoiselle Pélagie Van Tricasse, his cousin" (DOSE-016-008).
