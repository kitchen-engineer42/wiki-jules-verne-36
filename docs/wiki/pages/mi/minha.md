# Minha Garral

## Overview

She and her brother are "the grandchildren of the old Portuguese, became worthy of their grandfather, children worthy of Joam and Yaquita" (EHLA-003-029).

Her story ends in marriage and departure, when "Manoel and Minha Valdez, Lina and Fragoso, departed by one of the Amazon steamers" (EHLA-040-044) for Iquitos.

## Role in the Story

It is her wedding that occasions the whole journey, for "The marriage of Minha afforded an excellent opportunity" (EHLA-004-020) for the family to descend the river together.

Aboard the raft she keeps house, on whom with Lina "devolved the care of decorating the inside of the house" (EHLA-008-032), and she plays guide to her betrothed, as "Minha made Manoel admire the natural wonders which could not be found in their simplicity in the more civilized provinces of the east" (EHLA-007-040).

In her father's peril she labors with the others, "Benito, Manoel, and Minha tried all they could together to extract the secret from the document" (EHLA-035-001) that would save him.

## Character & Traits

She is devout and reverent before nature, murmuring that "such beautiful things are only lent to us" (EHLA-007-045).

She is serene and content, wishing "it would be very pleasant for us always to travel in this way, on this quiet water, shaded from the rays of the sun" (EHLA-018-026), though tender-hearted enough that on leaving home "Minha was a trifle sorrowful" (EHLA-006-037).

## Relationships

- [[Manoel Valdez]] — the young surgeon she is to wed; the tale opens with "Minha said to her betrothed" (EHLA-004-011) her wishes for their marriage.

- [[Joam Garral]] — her father, for whose sake she suffers; "Manoel had tried to reassure Minha by telling her that Judge Jarriquez was convinced of the innocence of Joam" (EHLA-036-028).

- [[Benito Garral]] — her brother, with whom "Benito and Minha responded with an affection unbounded and unceasing" (EHLA-003-039).

- Yaquita — her mother, whose call she heeds as "the voice of Yaquita was heard calling Minha into the house" (EHLA-010-033).

## Appearances

- [[Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]] — at the wedding "Minha, who looked most fascinating in her bride's costume" (EHLA-040-039) is given to Manoel.

- Earlier "Benito and Manoel had offered to accompany Minha through the thick woods which bordered the right bank of the Amazon" (EHLA-007-002), and at the denouement "Minha, Benito, Manoel, and Fragoso stood round him" (EHLA-039-004) as the cryptogram is read.
