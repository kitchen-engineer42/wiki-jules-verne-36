# The Death of Captain Nemo

## Overview

The Death of Captain Nemo closes the story of the commander first met in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas*, now revealed in *The Mysterious Island* as the colonists' secret benefactor. Having lived a dozen years on board the *Nautilus*, on board the *Nautilus* he desires to die (MI-059-009), amid the grotto that hides his vessel.

## What Happens

The colonists are summoned to the *Nautilus*, where the dying man waits, wishing to remain in the midst of those marvels which millions could not have purchased, to wait there for the death which was swiftly approaching (MI-059-002). He tells them his history and the secret of the island's coming ruin.

He gives his last instructions and names his tomb: "To-morrow I shall die, and I desire no other tomb than the *Nautilus*. It is my grave!" (MI-059-025). At his passing the colonists let the vessel sink into the abyss with his body aboard, and Cyrus Harding pronounces over him, "may the God of mercy have had pity on the soul of Captain Nemo, our benefactor" (MI-062-037).

## Significance

Nemo's death resolves the mystery that has guided the colonists' survival from the first, disclosing that the unseen hand which saved them again and again was that of the exile of *Twenty Thousand Leagues*. That he chose the *Nautilus* as his grave (MI-059-025) unites the two novels in a single figure, and Harding's benediction over Captain Nemo, our benefactor (MI-062-037) transforms the enigmatic outlaw of the sea into a redeemed protector.
