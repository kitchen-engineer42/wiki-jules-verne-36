# Mr. Ward

## Overview

He heads the federal police at Washington — "a man of about fifty years, of great power and intellect, was fully master of the important position he filled" (MW-002-008).

His authority extends over the service's means, being the officer "who held the government's funds" (MW-005-005).

## Role in the Story

He sets the pursuit of the mysterious inventor as the priority: "We are resolved by some means to establish communication with this inventor" (MW-008-028).

He reasons about the invention's danger — "this invention can be of no personal use to the man, that he should hide it from the rest of us" (MW-008-024) — and identifies the threatening letter, answering "Of which this is the original" (MW-010-029) when its facsimile is shown.

He also weighs operations carefully: "Mr. Ward did not think it worth while to dispatch me and my men to either point whence it had been reported" (MW-008-042).

## Character & Traits

He has a teasing streak; after the failed ascent "Mr. Ward was intent on rallying me about my unsuccess" (MW-008-015).

He is candid: "Mr. Ward frankly admitted that the whole matter seemed to him most extraordinary" (MW-005-026), yet cautious in inference, allowing an explanation only "though it is almost inadmissible, even impossible" (MW-010-039).

The case grips him — "Mr. Ward referred to the matter each time I saw him" (MW-006-029).

## Relationships

- [[John Strock]] — his trusted inspector, whom he generously exonerates after the Eyrie: "you accomplished everything that depended on human powers; and that no blame attaches to you" (MW-008-016), and welcomes back at the end with "you have come back; and that is the main thing" (MW-018-012).

- [[Robur]] — the inventor he is determined to capture: "it is much more important to place our hands on this fantastic inventor, who so constantly escapes us" (MW-008-018).

## Appearances

- [[The Master of the World]] — he arranges the naval support, "the two torpedo destroyers of which Mr. Ward had spoken" (MW-014-018), having earlier ensured "Before I left Washington Mr. Ward had informed me of their presence" (MW-011-050).

- His forecast proves true when, "as Mr. Ward had prophesied, I was the man of the hour" (MW-018-015).
