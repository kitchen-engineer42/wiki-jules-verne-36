# Mr. Bredejord

## Overview

He is a jurist of the first rank — "the Advocate Bredejord, one of the most illustrious lawyers of Stockholm" (WC-004-043).

His forensic gift is central to the mystery, for the investigation turns on "Mr. Bredejord's skill in questioning witnesses, and in drawing from them information which they themselves were scarcely aware of" (WC-009-013).

## Role in the Story

He is the doctor's contrarian, and their dispute over Erik's birth becomes a formal wager — "the bet between him and Mr. Bredejord" (WC-006-023).

He carries the inquiry abroad, since "Mr. Bredejord was introduced by the Swedish consul to the chief of police of New York" (WC-009-026), and lends his weight so that "Thanks to the influence of the doctor and Mr. Bredejord" (WC-011-057) the expedition is manned.

At the end his law secures Erik's fortune: "The latest legal business of Mr. Bredejord has been to establish Erik's claim as sole proprietor of the Vandalia mine" (WC-022-041).

## Character & Traits

He is a mordant wit whose barbs sting the doctor — the researches only "provoke the sarcasms of Mr. Bredejord, which were wounding, to the doctor's self-love" (WC-008-007) — and he punctuates his talk by "tapping his snuff-box with the ends of his fingers" (WC-004-055).

For all his coolness he is proud, once answering "more vexed than he was willing to appear to be" (WC-006-054) when the wager turned against him.

## Relationships

- [[Doctor Schwaryencrona]] — his oldest friend and opponent in endless debate, whom he twits with "you know very well you are only losing your time lecturing me" (WC-004-060) and challenges over the disputed Pliny, "you will finally see me in possession of your Pliny" (WC-006-060).

- [[Erik]] — the foundling whose cause he ultimately serves, rejoicing that "the rascality of Noah Jones has resulted in making our Erik one of the richest men in America" (WC-022-028).

- [[Tudor Brown]] — the villain whose forgeries he alone suspects, declaring "I suspect that the chart is the work of a rascal" (WC-014-034).

## Appearances

- [[The Waif of the Cynthia]] — where he braves the northern voyage though "The doctor and Mr. Bredejord both became a prey to seasickness, and descended to their cabins" (WC-013-040), and marvels at Erik's feat, "that they have failed completely during the last three centuries in this attempt that you have now accomplished" (WC-016-035).

- Long after, "Dr. Schwaryencrona, Mr. Bredejord, and Professor Hochstedt still play innumerable games of whist" (WC-022-045).
