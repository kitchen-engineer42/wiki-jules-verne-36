# Hurliguerly

## Overview

He is the ship's boatswain — "the boatswain of the _Halbrane_, a man named Hurliguerly, who came from the Isle of Wight" (AM-002-011).

He boasts of his standing with the captain, "I am the faithful companion of Captain Len Guy, who will listen to me willingly" (AM-002-019).

## Role in the Story

He is the go-between for Jeorling's passage, since "Atkins had told Hurliguerly on his arrival that I would take passage on the _Halbrane_" (AM-002-012), and when it is granted "The first person whom I met on the deck was Hurliguerly; he gave me a look of triumph" (AM-003-079).

He keeps the peace among the crew, as when "Hurliguerly pushed him towards Martin Holt, whose eyes expressed the liveliest gratitude" (AM-012-054), and his voice sways the men — "Did the crew intend to yield to Hurliguerly's argument" (AM-019-065).

## Character & Traits

He is a rogue with a warm heart, "Hurliguerly, a good fellow at bottom, as I afterwards learned" (AM-009-017), and fond of his glass, for once "Hurliguerly swallowed his last glass of whisky at a gulp" (AM-002-046).

Above all he loves to talk: "Hurliguerly regarded the opportunity as highly favourable for the renewal of talk with me" (AM-009-002).

## Relationships

- [[Jeorling]] — the passenger he befriends and banters with, assuring him "Their flesh is just as good as chicken, Mr. Jeorling" (AM-011-006).

- [[Len Guy]] — the captain he serves and counsels; at the crisis "Captain Len Guy, West, Hurliguerly, and I talked over the matter" (AM-022-005).

- Endicott — the ship's cook and his crony, the two alone cheering so that only "Hurliguerly the boatswain, and Endicott the cook" (AM-018-040) raised their voices.

## Appearances

- [[An Antarctic Mystery]] — where he braves the wreck, "it was Hurliguerly, working himself along with his hands" (AM-019-105) across the tilted deck, and greets each day cheerfully, for "Hurliguerly accosted me on the deck with a broad smile and a cheerful manner" (AM-012-004).

- As the drift bears them on he keeps his nerve — "Hurliguerly, who observed his comrades closely, appeared less anxious" (AM-022-051) — and jokes, "If the current goes to the pole, we shall go there; and if it doesn't, we shan't" (AM-020-217).
