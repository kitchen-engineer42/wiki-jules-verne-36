# Joel Hansen

## Overview

He is plainly esteemed: "A worthy lad was Joel Hansen" (TN-003-011).

By trade a mountain guide, "Joel, being naturally very pleasant and obliging, was known and loved in every village in the Telemark" (TN-003-014).

## Role in the Story

Guiding is his calling, and "his vocation of guide gave him considerable leisure at this season of the year" (TN-005-048); on the road "Joel guided the vehicle with no little skill" (TN-008-004).

It is he who first lays the matter before their guest, for "Joel then proceeded to relate Ole Kamp's whole history" (TN-011-001).

He defends his sister's inheritance against the usurer, insisting "This ticket belonged originally to our cousin, Ole Kamp, and had not Ole Kamp a perfect right to bequeath it to his betrothed" (TN-014-065).

## Character & Traits

He is powerfully built and fearless — noted for "the great strength that enabled him to endure the embraces of these formidable animals, and to the imperturbable coolness" (TN-003-012) in the hunt.

His bond with his sister is absolute: "My sister and I are one" (TN-014-081), though his temper runs hot, so that against Sandgoist "Joel made a violent effort to control himself" (TN-014-049).

## Relationships

- [[Hulda Hansen]] — his beloved sister, whom he steadies again and again: "There is no cause for anxiety, little sister" (TN-005-053).

- [[Ole Kamp]] — his cousin and comrade, for "A close friendship gradually sprung up between Joel and Ole" (TN-004-007) before the sailor's fatal voyage.

- [[Sylvius Hogg]] — the professor he saved and served; the tale recalls "the intrepid manner in which Joel and his sister had risked their own lives to save him" (TN-013-032).

- Siegfrid Helmboe — his own betrothed of Bamble, at whose side he appears as "Joel, her affianced husband" (TN-020-025).

## Appearances

- [[Ticket No. 9672]] — where he rescues the stranded professor on the Maristien, calling out "My sister is also coming to help you" (TN-008-060).

- On the journey to Bamble, room lacking, "the brave fellow trudged along on foot at the horse's head" (TN-016-001), and at Christiania Sylvius presents him — "this is Joel, her noble brother" (TN-019-017).
