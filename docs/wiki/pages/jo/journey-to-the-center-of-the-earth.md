## Overview

*A Journey to the Center of the Earth* is Jules Verne's subterranean adventure, narrated by young [[Axel]], nephew of the impetuous geologist Professor Liedenbrock. It opens as "my uncle, Professor Liedenbrock, rushed into his little house, No. 19 Königstrasse" (JCE-001-002) with a discovery that will send them underground.

That discovery is a runic cipher decoded in [[Deciphering the Saknussemm Cipher]], directing a bold traveller to descend a volcano in [[Iceland]].

## Plot Summary

The cipher's message is a command: "Descend, bold traveller, into the crater of the jokul of Sneffels, which the shadow of Scartaris touches before the kalends of July... and thou wilt attain the centre of the earth" (JCE-005-050).

Liedenbrock resolves at once to "Penetrate to the centre of the earth!" (JCE-006-002). With Axel and the Icelandic guide Hans, he descends the crater into a vast subterranean world of caverns, a great sea, and prehistoric life, before a volcanic eruption hurls the party back to the surface.

## Main Characters

- [[Axel]] — the narrator and reluctant nephew
- Professor Liedenbrock — the headstrong geologist who leads the descent
- Hans — the steadfast Icelandic guide

## Key Places & Technology

- [[Iceland]] — where the descent into Sneffels begins
- [[Deciphering the Saknussemm Cipher]] — the coded message that launches the journey

## Themes

The novel dramatizes the heroic geology of Verne's age, plunging its characters through the strata of the earth into a lost prehistoric world. It sets youthful caution against scientific obsession, with Axel's fear balanced by Liedenbrock's unstoppable will to know.
