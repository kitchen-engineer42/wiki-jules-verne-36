# Robur

## Overview

Robur is the enigmatic engineer whose command of heavier-than-air flight makes him one of Verne's great defiant inventors. He first announces himself simply — "Robur," replied Uncle Prudent (RC-003-027) — and returns years later to declare, with characteristic pride, "I, Robur — Robur, the Conqueror!" (MW-015-053). Across two novels he embodies genius unbound by law or society.

## Role in the Story

In *Robur the Conqueror* he seizes the members of the [[Weldon Institute]] aboard his airship, the *Albatross*, whose crew of eight men all told he commands (RC-007-032), to prove his doctrine of flight.

He reappears in *Master of the World* as the builder of a still greater machine; when at last unmasked, the recognition is total — "Robur, the Conqueror! This then was..." (MW-016-002) — and his reckless ambition drives him to destruction.

## Character & Traits

Robur is genius fused with defiance. He proclaims that man shall become master of atmospherical space by apparatus heavier than the air (RC-004-015), staking his identity on a scientific creed the world rejects.

His pride is absolute, crystallized in the name he claims for himself (MW-015-053), and it is this same pride — refusing all restraint — that turns visionary into menace by the second novel.
