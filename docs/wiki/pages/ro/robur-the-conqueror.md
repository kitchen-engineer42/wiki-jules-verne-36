## Overview

*Robur the Conqueror* is Jules Verne's great aviation romance, pitting the visionary engineer [[Robur]] against the balloon-devoted [[Weldon Institute]] of Philadelphia. Robur champions heavier-than-air flight, staking his creed on his airship the [[Albatross]].

He proclaims that man shall become "master of atmospherical space by apparatus heavier than the air" (RC-004-015) — a doctrine the Institute's members flatly reject.

## Plot Summary

Robur bursts into the Institute's hall and, mocked, abducts its president and secretary aboard the Albatross, "a framework a hundred feet long" lifted by dozens of screws and manned by a crew of eight (RC-007-032).

He carries his captives around the world to prove the superiority of the aeroplane over the balloon. The prisoners escape and complete their rival balloon, the *Go-Ahead*, only for Robur to reappear and vanish — his ambition carried further in the sequel *[[Master of the World]]*, where he builds the machine [[The Terror]].

## Main Characters

- [[Robur]] — the defiant engineer and master of the Albatross
- Uncle Prudent — president of the Weldon Institute and Robur's chief antagonist
- Phil Evans — the Institute's secretary, abducted alongside Prudent

## Key Places & Technology

- [[Albatross]] — Robur's heavier-than-air flying machine
- [[Weldon Institute]] — the Philadelphia balloon club Robur challenges

## Themes

The novel dramatizes the historic contest between "lighter than air" and "heavier than air" flight, and Verne sides emphatically with the winged machine. In Robur he sketches the ambivalent genius — a benefactor of science whose pride shades into menace, fully realized only in the sequel.
