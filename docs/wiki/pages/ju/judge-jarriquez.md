# Judge Jarriquez

## Overview

He is a solitary scholar of the law: "Jarriquez, who was a confirmed old bachelor, never left his law-books" (EHLA-024-004), and a past master at chess.

As magistrate at Manaos he takes charge of the case, so that "Judge Jarriquez made his way to the prison in God-the-Son Street, where the convict had been placed" (EHLA-024-009).

## Role in the Story

The cipher is precisely his domain, for on its discovery "Jarriquez suddenly found himself face to face with the study of which he was a master" (EHLA-032-021).

He gives himself wholly to the puzzle — "Judge Jarriquez had all the time been absorbed in working at the puzzle" (EHLA-033-001) — and his motive shifts, so that "he now really set to work more in the interest of Joam Dacosta than for the satisfaction of his analytical faculties" (EHLA-034-007).

At the last, in a rush, "By means of the number Judge Jarriquez interpreted the whole of the cryptogram" (EHLA-039-016).

## Character & Traits

He is stern and unshakable — "Judge Jarriquez was evidently hard of heart, and proof against all surprise" (EHLA-024-071) — and at first biased, being "naturally prepossessed against all criminals" (EHLA-024-054).

Yet he is brilliantly acute, applying himself "with truly remarkable sagacity, which denoted a very observant mind" (EHLA-032-050).

## Relationships

- [[Joam Garral]] — the accused whose innocence he comes to accept, since "The reasoning of Joam Dacosta was unanswerable, and Judge Jarriquez felt it to be so" (EHLA-025-036).

- [[Manoel Valdez]] — who appeals to him for help; "Manoel then presented himself at the abode of Judge Jarriquez, and was immediately admitted" (EHLA-036-039).

- [[Torres]] — the dead blackmailer whose word he doubts, demanding "who told you, after all, that Torres did not tell a lie" (EHLA-033-079).

## Appearances

- [[Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]] — where the cipher nearly breaks him: "Judge Jarriquez had worked himself into such a state of exasperation that there really was some fear that his mental faculties would lose their balance" (EHLA-034-052), until at the crucial clue "Judge Jarriquez, quite frightful to look upon, devoured the lines of the document with a fixed stare" (EHLA-038-060).

- He reads out the solution as "Judge Jarriquez sat down on a stone seat" (EHLA-039-004), and that evening "Judge Jarriquez dined with the family on board the giant raft, and when evening came he shook hands with them all" (EHLA-039-044).
