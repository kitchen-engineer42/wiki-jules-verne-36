# James Playfair

## Overview

The steamer Dolphin belongs to "James Playfair, a fine young man of thirty, the boldest skipper of the British merchant marine" (BR-001-016).

At first he is a hard man of business who runs the blockade for gain, one who "had a grudge against those who had caused this war" (BR-004-036).

## Role in the Story

The Glasgow cotton-famine inspires his venture: "It was the sight of this great misery which had put the idea of his bold enterprise into James Playfair's head" (BR-001-033).

He declares "I will take the command of this steamer, I will defy all the ships of the Federal marine for speed, and I will run the blockade of one of the southern ports" (BR-001-042).

He fixes on the hardest target, for "I think of going straight to Charleston" (BR-001-052).

Discovering a woman among the crew, he gives up his quarters: "This cabin will henceforward belong to Miss Jenny Halliburtt" (BR-003-074).

Transformed by love, he vows to free her father: "in two hours your father will be in safety near you, or I shall have perished in endeavouring to save him!" (BR-008-069).

The tale ends in "the marriage of James Playfair, of the firm of Vincent Playfair & Co., of Glasgow, with Miss Jenny Halliburtt, of Boston" (BR-010-006).

## Character & Traits

He casts the venture in commercial terms, resolving "to carry out his plan by way of exchange, and to make his proposition under the guise of a commercial enterprise" (BR-001-035).

His early severity is real; of the stowaway he orders "fifty lashes with the cat-o'-nine-tails to loosen his tongue" (BR-003-045).

He professes a mercenary creed: "I am a merchant, and as such I only occupy myself with the interests of my house" (BR-005-056).

Yet his seamanship is masterly, for "James Playfair was wonderfully well acquainted with all the secrets of Charleston Bay, and he guided his ship through the darkness with an unerring hand" (BR-009-050).

## Relationships

- Jenny Halliburtt — the passenger, disguised as a boy, for love of whom he is remade; "James knew that a profound, imperious, irresistible love had taken possession of his whole being" (BR-006-004).

- Crockston — the sailor who smuggles Jenny aboard, whom James takes on with a warning: "bring your nephew, but if I don't find the uncle the hearty fellow he pretends to be, he will have some business with me" (BR-002-045).

- Vincent Playfair — his uncle and shipowner, who at parting bids him "go quickly, and come back quicker still; above all, don't abuse your position" (BR-002-052).

## Appearances

- [[The Blockade Runners]] — in which, running the Charleston blockade, "James Playfair had taken his place at the rudder, and with a steady hand he guided his boat towards White Point" (BR-008-084).
