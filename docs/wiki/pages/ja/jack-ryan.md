# Jack Ryan

## Overview

He is a leading spirit of the old pit — "In the first rank of the believers in the supernatural in the Dochart pit figured Jack Ryan, Harry's friend" (UC-005-002).

He is robust and full of life, "A hearty fellow like Jack Ryan, with so strong a constitution" (UC-009-034).

## Role in the Story

His old craft makes him invaluable, for "Jack Ryan had not forgotten his old mining habits, and he was well acquainted with the Dochart pit" (UC-009-048), so that "guided by Jack Ryan, the party set out for the Aberfoyle mines" (UC-009-058) to seek the missing engineer.

In peril he does not falter — "Jack Ryan, bravely following their example, quickly overtook the head-most of the party" (UC-009-073) — and at the climax "It was Jack Ryan, fully determined to reach the madman before he could do the dreadful deed of destruction" (UC-018-029).

## Character & Traits

He is forever singing, and once "Jack Ryan, singing as he went, soon disappeared in the heights of the shaft" (UC-003-068); his cheer is proverbial, for he is known for "all his good humor and usually infectious merriment" (UC-011-001), being "as joyous as a lark rising in the first beams of the sun" (UC-014-005).

He is fondly superstitious, though in time "Jack Ryan had been obliged to admit that his belief in hobgoblins was in a measure weakened" (UC-012-035).

## Relationships

- Harry Ford — his closest friend, whose ear knows his voice anywhere; Harry declares a song could come "from the lungs of any man but Jack Ryan" (UC-003-043).

- [[James Starr]] — the engineer he helps and follows, when "James Starr, Harry, and Jack Ryan were about to lead Nell's steps over yet untrodden paths" (UC-014-002).

- [[Nell]] — the mysterious girl he cherishes, crying "Our little Nell is too good to lose" (UC-014-052).

## Appearances

- [[The Underground City]] — where he takes up "his abode in Simon Ford's new cottage" (UC-010-014), and in the crisis is saved when Harry, "swimming towards Jack Ryan, he dived repeatedly" (UC-018-038).

- At the great festival "Jack Ryan, in his favorite character of piper, and in all the glory of full dress, blew up his chanter" (UC-019-002).
