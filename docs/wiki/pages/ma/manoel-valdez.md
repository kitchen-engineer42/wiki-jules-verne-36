# Manoel Valdez

## Overview

He introduces himself on the hunt where the story opens as "Manoel, assistant surgeon, Brazilian army" (EHLA-002-074).

A close contemporary of Benito Garral, "Manoel, born in 1832, was one year older than Benito" (EHLA-003-033).

## Role in the Story

When first met, "Manoel Valdez had already obtained his first step, and he had come away on leave for some months to the fazenda" (EHLA-003-034), where his affection for Minha ripens until "Manoel had opened his heart to Benito" (EHLA-004-002).

In the crisis he helps clear Joam, presenting the recovered case to the judge as the proof that "the man who gives it to you is he who took it from the body of Torres" (EHLA-031-050).

At the story's close "Manoel Valdez almost immediately sent in his resignation, so as to join the family at Iquitos, where he is still following the profession of a country doctor" (EHLA-040-041).

## Character & Traits

Where Benito is impetuous, "his friend Manoel, who was more serious and reflective" (EHLA-003-040) balances him.

He is a shrewd judge of character, "Manoel, who had taken a great dislike to the man and only waited for an opportunity to declare it" (EHLA-016-002) — of the schemer Torres.

He is brave and self-sacrificing: at the duel "Manoel by a last effort wished to insist on fighting him instead of Benito" (EHLA-026-089).

## Relationships

- Minha Garral — his betrothed, for from the first "Manoel was in love with his daughter" (EHLA-023-007), as her father perceives.

- [[Benito Garral]] — his dearest friend and future brother-in-law: "We are brothers, Benito" (EHLA-022-044).

- [[Joam Garral]] — his prospective father-in-law, whom he never doubts, so that "his misfortunes made him even dearer and more devoted to him" (EHLA-020-102).

- [[Torres]] — the blackmailer he detests, at whose fatal duel "The signal was given by Manoel, and Torres and Benito stepped forward" (EHLA-026-096).

## Appearances

- [[Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]] — where his surgeon's skill saves Benito after the river dive, for "in a few minutes Manoel's efforts restored him to life" (EHLA-031-011), and where he steadies the grieving youth, "Manoel supporting him, for the unfortunate young man had hardly strength to support himself" (EHLA-035-030).

- The voyage ends in "the marriage of Manoel and Minha" (EHLA-040-034).
