# Martin Paz

## Overview

He is "a handsome young man, wearing with unparalleled nobility the national costume of the Indian of the mountains" (PL-002-029).

Though of the conquered race, "the proud bearing of the Indian suited the chivalric hidalgo" who befriends him (PL-004-057).

He is an outcast, "the poor Indian, unknown and proscribed" who must renounce all hope of happiness (PL-006-010).

## Role in the Story

Having struck down his rival, "Martin Paz, thanks to the darkness and the rapidity of his flight, may hope to escape his pursuers" (PL-002-067).

To the Spaniard who shelters him he owns his deed: "I am pursued by soldiers for having defended myself against a mestizo who attacked me, and levelled him to the ground with a blow from my poignard" (PL-004-016).

He is bound to his people's cause, foreseeing "the day when my brethren shall rise _en masse_ is perhaps not far distant" (PL-004-062).

In the rising, "at the head of one of the columns, Martin Paz was waving the black flag--the flag of independence" (PL-008-018).

In the final struggle "Martin Paz, with supernatural strength, turned against the mestizo the poignard and the arm which held it, and plunged it into his heart" (PL-008-047).

## Character & Traits

He survives the torrent of the Rimac by "his surprising strength, his insurmountable will, and especially his sublime coolness" (PL-004-002).

His pride is fierce; insulted, "in a first impulse of indignation, raised his arm to strike him; but turning his weapon against himself" (PL-004-012).

The rebel Sambo works upon him, having "roused the most sensitive chords of his proud nature to vibrate" (PL-007-063).

## Relationships

- Sarah — the merchant's ward he loves though "she was beyond the reach of his heart; but he forgot all these impossibilities in his all-absorbing passion" (PL-002-031), and for whom "the young Indian protected her and risked his life for her" (PL-005-048).

- Don Vegal — the noble Spanish marquis who shelters him, and who "left the Indian, moved to tears by a confidence so generous; he yielded himself entirely to the protection of the marquis" (PL-004-020).

## Appearances

- [[The Pearl of Lima]] — at whose tragic close "Suddenly an arrow whistles through the air, and pierces the heart of Martin Paz" (PL-009-065), so that he and Sarah are, "in their brief and final re-union, betrothed for eternity" (PL-009-071).
