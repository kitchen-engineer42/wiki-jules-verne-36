# André Letourneur

## Overview

He appears in the passenger list as "M. Letourneur and his son Andre, Frenchmen, of Havre" (SC2-002-006).

The narrator introduces him fully: "Andre Letourneur is about twenty years of age, with a gentle, interesting countenance, but, to the irrepressible grief of his father, is a hopeless cripple" (SC2-004-004).

His lameness usually needs support, though at times "Andre could get along tolerably well without the assistance of an arm" (SC2-018-012).

## Role in the Story

Despite his infirmity he does his share, the survivors "including even Andre Letourneur, have been taking our turn at the pumps" (SC2-019-006).

On the raft "Andre Letourneur is the life of our party" (SC2-032-010).

In one struggle he saves the narrator, for "Andre Letourneur had caught hold of one of his legs, and thus saved my life" (SC2-038-021).

When the drawing of the death-lots threatens his father, he shields him, "throwing his arms about his father" (SC2-055-008).

Even after fainting in that scene, "As soon as Andre Letourneur came to his senses, his first thought was for his father" (SC2-054-009).

## Character & Traits

At the first alarm of fire "the young man preserved an admirable composure, and begged his father not to be alarmed" (SC2-011-013).

His temper is gentle and pious: "Andre smiled; then, in a more serious tone, he expressed his gratitude for the timely help that had been vouchsafed us in our hour of need" (SC2-018-009).

He rallies the despairing, urging that "let us keep up our courage while we can" (SC2-032-006).

He is cultivated and quick, observing that "poets are like proverbs; you can always find one to contradict another" (SC2-005-017).

## Relationships

- M. Letourneur, his father, whose whole life is bound up in him, shields him bodily — "M. Letourneur placed himself in front of Andre to shelter him from the waves" (SC2-035-004).

- [[Robert Curtis]] — the mate he befriends, who is "always ready to assist and amuse young Letourneur, who evidently enjoys his company" (SC2-004-016).

- [[Miss Herbey]] — the young lady who at the last, having "consented to become a member of their family, and finds in M. Letourneur a father, and in Andre a brother" (SC2-057-016).

## Appearances

- [[The Survivors of the Chancellor]] — at whose rescue all rejoice, "with the exception of Andre and his father, who remained by themselves together at the stern" (SC2-057-002).
