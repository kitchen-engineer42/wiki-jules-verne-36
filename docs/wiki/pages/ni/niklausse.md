# Niklausse

## Overview

He is the commune counsellor of Quiquendone, coupled always with the burgomaster, so that "The Counsellor Niklausse and the Burgomaster Van Tricasse at last knew what it was to have an agitated night" (DOSE-005-002).

He is that burgomaster's perfect echo, for "Counsellor Niklausse was incapable of originating any objection to the burgomaster's opinion" (DOSE-002-011).

## Role in the Story

Doctor Ox's oxygen stirs him first through speech, when "Niklausse himself experienced a kind of irresistible desire to talk" (DOSE-005-029).

His lifelong deference then collapses, and "the Counsellor Niklausse, forgetful of all that he owed to his superior, to the supreme magistrate of the town, pushed Van Tricasse violently back" (DOSE-013-029).

Away from the gas-works the spell fades: "Little by little, when they had taken twenty steps on their road, the worthy notables grew more calm" (DOSE-005-060).

In the end the projected war evaporates, for "it was because the burgomaster and the counsellor could not make up their minds to decide upon it" (DOSE-016-005).

## Character & Traits

After ten years he still cannot decide, confessing "We have been talking over this grave matter for ten years" (DOSE-002-005).

His phlegm is total: "The counsellor nodded his head slowly in token of assent; then he remained silent for nearly half an hour" (DOSE-002-009).

Even leaving a room is ponderous, since "Niklausse's preparations for departure consumed at least a quarter of an hour" (DOSE-003-046).

Under the oxygen he flares up, crying "It requires but a spark to inflame a Fleming!" (DOSE-005-048).

In the quarrel he grows as belligerent as the burgomaster, "equally ready for a tussle" (DOSE-013-014).

When the gas is gone the old torpor returns, and the two friends resume "putting, as formerly, long pauses between their questions and answers" (DOSE-013-047).

## Relationships

- [[Van Tricasse]] — his bosom friend and fellow-magistrate, whom the oxygen turns into an antagonist, so that "a hostile meeting was about to take place between the Burgomaster Van Tricasse and the Counsellor Niklausse" (DOSE-013-077).

- [[Doctor Ox]] — whose lighting scheme he helps sanction, and whom, once inflamed, he menaces, when "the two notables, with folded arms and bristling air, confronted Doctor Ox, ready to do him some violence" (DOSE-005-055).

- Frantz, his son, is "the betrothed lover of Suzel" (DOSE-006-002), the burgomaster's daughter, binding the two families.

## Appearances

- [[Doctor Ox's Experiment]] — in which, at the town theatre, "not far off were the Counsellor Niklausse and his family, not forgetting the amorous Frantz" (DOSE-007-025).
