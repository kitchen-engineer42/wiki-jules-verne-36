# Penellan

## Overview

He is "Penellan the Breton, who replaced Pierre Nouquet as helmsman" of the Jeune-Hardie (WAI-002-019).

A seasoned polar hand, "Penellan, who had already engaged in whale-fishing in the arctic waters" saw to the outfitting of the expedition (WAI-003-030).

## Role in the Story

Through the wintering-over "Penellan supervised the whole" (WAI-007-006).

For the sledge-search "Penellan, aided by Marie, was preparing the clothing necessary for the expedition" (WAI-008-008).

His resourcefulness serves them again and again: "It occurred to Penellan to make use of the chafing-dish to melt the ice in the direction they wanted" (WAI-010-045).

When fuel runs short he proposes "to burn what we can of the brig, from the barricading to the water-line" (WAI-013-017).

He meets the polar bears head-on, for "Penellan appeared, loaded his pistol, and discharged it in the bear's ear" (WAI-015-041).

## Character & Traits

He is tireless, "sustained by an indomitable courage" (WAI-009-009).

Against the scurvy "Penellan also strongly advised cold ablutions every morning" (WAI-007-013).

He keeps up the others' spirits, urging that "we ought to continue to pierce the wall where we have begun" (WAI-010-048).

His valour never flags; "Penellan precipitated himself upon Vasling" in the final struggle (WAI-015-011).

## Relationships

- [[Jean Cornbutte]] — the captain he serves, who "had a devoted sailor in Penellan the Breton, who had long been his fellow-voyager" (WAI-002-022).

- Marie — the captain's niece, in whom he trusts, saying "The little one has force and courage, and will be our guardian angel" (WAI-003-021).

- André Vasling — the treacherous mate; "The mate, therefore, cordially detested the helmsman, who returned his dislike heartily" (WAI-007-010), until Penellan at last confronts him, "throwing himself, cutlass in hand, on the mate" (WAI-014-013).

## Appearances

- [[A Winter Amid the Ice]] — through the terrible polar winter "Penellan, Pierre Nouquet, Turquiette, Fidèle Misonne, did the work of ten sailors" (WAI-016-020).
