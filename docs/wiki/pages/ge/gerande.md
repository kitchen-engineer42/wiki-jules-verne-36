# Gérande

## Overview

She is the daughter of "the old clockmaker, Master Zacharius" (MZ-001-004), in old Geneva.

To her ailing father she is "his young and lovely daughter" (MZ-003-009), whom he dreads to leave without support.

## Role in the Story

She prays for all the world, and "more earnestly than all for the unknown misfortunes of her father" (MZ-001-039).

As folk whisper of sorcery, "These rumours reached Gerande, and she often trembled for her father" (MZ-002-003).

She tends him with gentle care, and "Gerande led her father towards the more pleasant promenades of the town" (MZ-003-008).

She labours for his very soul, so that "the young girl undertook her father's conversion; and her influence was so effective that the old watchmaker promised to attend high mass" (MZ-004-024).

When at last he vanishes she does not falter: "Let us find my father!" cried Gerande (MZ-005-007).

## Character & Traits

She is a girl of eighteen whose "oval face recalled that of the artless Madonnas" (MZ-001-017).

Her piety is unaffected: "Gerande went to her accustomed bench, and kneeled with profound and simple reverence" (MZ-004-033).

To save him "Gerande resolved to call religion to her aid" (MZ-004-023).

Yet she is clear-eyed in her grief, for "Gerande did not deceive herself; her father's body and soul were for ever lost" (MZ-005-002).

## Relationships

- Aubert Thün — the apprentice she loves, whose memory alone binds her to life: "Poor Gerande would have lost her life with that of her father, had it not been for the thought of Aubert, who still attached her to the world" (MZ-003-002).

- Master Zacharius — her father, whose mechanistic denial of the soul she answers from the heart: "I would not love you as I do, father" (MZ-002-034).

- Together with Aubert she strives for the old man's soul, and "Aubert and Gerande knelt by the old man's bed-side and prayed together" (MZ-004-049).

## Appearances
