# Jenny Halliburtt

## Overview

Her whole aim is her imprisoned father, for "Miss Halliburtt was standing on the poop, looking at the town where her father was kept prisoner, and her eyes filled with tears" (BR-006-035).

A daughter of the North, "She loved her country, she was zealous in the great cause of the Union" (BR-005-051).

## Role in the Story

Her cause reshapes the whole voyage, so that "the Captain seemed to have decided to undertake everything in his power to deliver Miss Jenny's father" (BR-005-062).

Through the rescue "Miss Jenny, sitting at the poop of the Dolphin, was anxiously waiting the Captain's return" (BR-008-002).

At the moment her father is freed "the young girl uttered a piercing cry, and fell unconscious on the deck" (BR-008-067).

Then at last "Jenny fell into her father's arms" (BR-009-020).

## Character & Traits

She is fearless, declaring "I was not brought up to be fearful; they accustom us to everything in America" (BR-005-031).

James finds "her to be a young girl, calm, strong, thoughtful, and intelligent" (BR-005-050).

Yet "the young girl kept a perfect reserve, and her secret remained deeply buried in her heart" (BR-006-008).

For her father she suffers in silence, for "Jenny, without saying anything about it, was a prey to incessant fears" (BR-008-061).

Her courage is proven in the tempest, when "Miss Halliburtt had passed the worst hours of the storm at his side" (BR-006-004).

Her warmth is as ready as her courage, "pressing the American's rough hands between her small white palms" (BR-008-025).

## Relationships

- [[James Playfair]] — the Dolphin's captain, remade by love of her and her cause, who at the last presents her as "his brave young wife" (BR-010-013).

- Mr. Halliburtt, her imprisoned father, is the whole object of her voyage; of him she says "he little thinks his daughter is so near him" (BR-007-006).

- Crockston — the devoted sailor who smuggled her aboard, whom she calls "the best man on earth" (BR-008-051).

## Appearances

- [[The Blockade Runners]] — at whose close "There was Miss Jenny in bridal array and beside her the worthy Crockston" (BR-010-005).
