# Jean Cornbutte

## Overview

He is "a sailor of some sixty years, but still vigorous and sturdy, with, an open, honest countenance" (WAI-001-003).

A veteran master, "After having long commanded the vessels of a rich shipowner of Havre, he had settled down in his native town" (WAI-001-019), and had the brig Jeune-Hardie built at his own expense.

## Role in the Story

When his ship comes home without his son, he refuses to mourn, resolving "I am going to discharge the cargo with all speed, make up my crew, and sail in search of my son" (WAI-002-014).

Taking the command himself, "He alone replaced his son in the command of the brig" (WAI-002-019).

Beset by the polar ice, "Jean Cornbutte made his preliminary preparations for wintering" (WAI-006-006).

To press the search over the floes "Jean Cornbutte held a council to settle the plan of his operations" (WAI-008-002).

He finds his son alive but does not outlast the winter: "Jean Cornbutte was buried on the coast. He had left his native land to find his son, and had died in these terrible regions!" (WAI-016-006).

## Character & Traits

He will not believe his son dead, insisting "but it is also possible that he saved himself" (WAI-002-016).

His resolve is pious; he vows "by the help of God, I will bring your lover back to you!" (WAI-002-040).

He is "resolved to advance as far as possible" into the polar seas (WAI-005-021).

To keep his men from despair "Jean Cornbutte did not forget to have readings and prayers" (WAI-007-014).

## Relationships

- Louis Cornbutte, his son and the lost mate for whom the whole voyage is undertaken, wrings from him "an accent of the most profound despair" when feared dead (WAI-011-050).

- Marie, his niece and ward and Louis's betrothed, was left in his charge: "Her mother, when she was dying, had confided her to her brother, Jean Cornbutte" (WAI-001-020).

- Penellan, his loyal helmsman, is "a devoted sailor in Penellan the Breton, who had long been his fellow-voyager" (WAI-002-022).

- André Vasling, the treacherous mate who covets Marie, sails under him after "Jean Cornbutte again offered André Vasling his old rank on board" (WAI-002-020).

## Appearances

- [[A Winter Amid the Ice]] — in which the old captain at last succumbs to scurvy in the ice: "Louis Cornbutte approached his father's bedside; the old man was almost motionless, and his limbs were helpless from disease" (WAI-014-004).
