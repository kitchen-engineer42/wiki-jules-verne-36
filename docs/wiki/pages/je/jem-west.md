# Jem West

## Overview

He, not the captain, is the man the crew truly heed — "the exceptionally able man whom they all obeyed at a word, a gesture" (AM-004-003).

A sailor to the marrow, "James West was born on the sea, and had passed his childhood on board a lighter belonging to his father" (AM-004-004), so wholly one with the schooner that "if the _Halbrane_ had a heart it was in James West's breast that it beat" (AM-004-007).

## Role in the Story

The captain trusts him with everything, since "Captain Len Guy left the working of the ship to James West" (AM-006-001), and "His lieutenant, James West, would go whithersoever he was ordered to go" (AM-007-002).

His vigilance guards them among the ice — "West had a cask fixed at the head of the foremast" (AM-013-024) for the lookout — and in danger "As for West, whom nothing could divert from his duty, he repeated his commands" (AM-019-007).

## Character & Traits

He is without vanity or greed, for "James West was not even ambitious of a higher rise; he did not want to make a fortune" (AM-004-005).

He is famously sparing of speech — the narrator marvels once that "West had honoured me by so lengthy a reply" (AM-006-024) — and "West, who was generally so self-restrained" (AM-017-172) holds his temper even under insult; "West also was silent" (AM-020-180).

## Relationships

- [[Len Guy]] — his captain, who leans on him utterly, having "handed over the command of the schooner to West" (AM-020-014).

- [[Jeorling]] — the passenger to whom he unbends only rarely, granting the narrator that memorable "so lengthy a reply" noted above.

- [[Dirk Peters]] — a fellow of iron labor, for "I worked with the captain and West at this onerous task, and Dirk Peters also turned up and lent the valuable assistance of his great strength" (AM-022-048).

## Appearances

- [[An Antarctic Mystery]] — where amid the ice "West was watching closely in order to avoid collisions, and did not leave the deck even for an instant" (AM-019-076).

- When mutiny breaks out he faces it without flinching, crying "stop there, or we fire on the first who goes a step farther" (AM-020-138), and at the last "West shouldered his gun and fired; a sailor dropped into the bottom of the boat" (AM-022-075).
