# Jeorling

## Overview

He is an American of New England — addressed as "Mr. Jeorling, of Connecticut" (AM-007-038).

He knows Poe's tale intimately, for "I had read Poe's romance over and over again, I was curious" (AM-004-035) about its truth.

A solitary man past his youth, he owns that "I am not likely to take a fancy for marrying at forty" (AM-001-022).

## Role in the Story

He sets out as a passenger, since "My intention was to take my passage on board the _Halbrane_ so soon as she should come to her moorings in Christmas Harbour" (AM-001-039), and the captain later welcomes him, "I congratulate myself upon having you for a passenger until our arrival at the Falklands" (AM-008-015).

His berth was secured by the boatswain's intercession — "I succeeded in bringing the captain to change his mind about you" (AM-006-004) — and as a level-headed American he is pressed on the central question, "whether you think everything in Arthur Pym's journal, which has been published by Edgar Poe, is exactly true" (AM-008-047).

## Character & Traits

By temperament he is a rationalist, confessing that "for a practical man of unimaginative temperament, I felt strangely excited" (AM-008-006) as the mystery deepened, until "I who accused Captain Guy of being insane" (AM-006-047) found his own certainties overturned.

He tells his tale with dry irony, noting he speaks "I am talking of the Weddell route as though it were a macadamized road, well kept, with mile-stones" (AM-013-002).

## Relationships

- [[Len Guy]] — the Halbrane's captain, who presses him: "are you now convinced of the reality of the voyage of the _Jane_" (AM-008-018).

- Hurliguerly — the garrulous boatswain he befriends, "the boatswain of the _Halbrane_, a man named Hurliguerly, who came from the Isle of Wight" (AM-002-011).

- [[Dirk Peters]] — the half-breed of Pym's story, whose reality astounds him: "This Dirk Peters had really existed" (AM-004-047).

## Appearances

- [[An Antarctic Mystery]] — where he shares the ship's perils, as when a storm strikes and "a terrific lurch of the ship to starboard under the stroke of a mountainous wave, flung everything on the deck into wild confusion" (AM-012-038), and takes his turn at the helm, "I pride myself on being a good steersman" (AM-011-030).

- One night "I was sleeping--at two hours after midnight--and was awakened by a plaintive and continuous murmuring sound" (AM-014-013).
