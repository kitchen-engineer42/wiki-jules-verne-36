# Fritz Napoleon Smith

## Overview

He is "his remote descendant, Fritz Napoleon Smith, who, after thirty generations have come and gone, is owner of the same newspaper which his ancestor established" (YEAR-001-004).

His fortune is scarcely conceivable: "Smith's wealth went on growing, till now it reaches the almost unimaginable figure of $10,000,000,000" (YEAR-001-007).

He drives himself without pause — "His labors are incessant" (YEAR-001-008).

## Role in the Story

He transformed the old paper: "Mr. Fritz Napoleon Smith bought it for a mere trifle, and originated telephonic journalism" (YEAR-001-005), so that "Every one is familiar with Fritz Napoleon Smith's system" (YEAR-001-006).

His inventive reach is everywhere — the sky-advertising too, for "This, too, is one of Mr. Fritz Napoleon Smith's ideas" (YEAR-001-039), and beyond journalism "Mr. Smith has obtained a lease of the great falls of Niagara" (YEAR-001-069).

The tale traces one day of his rounds, until "Mr. Smith's daily tour through the several departments of his newspaper is now finished" (YEAR-001-041).

## Character & Traits

The day opens ill: "This morning Mr. Fritz Napoleon Smith awoke in very bad humor" (YEAR-001-010).

He is efficient to the bone — "Mr. Smith, being above all a practical man; has reduced the problem of existence to its simplest terms" (YEAR-001-060) — yet not without a softer side, for once "Entranced by the harmony, forgetful of the hour, Smith did not notice that it was growing dark" (YEAR-001-090).

## Relationships

- Mrs. Edith Smith — his wife, often in Paris, with whom, despite the difference of hours, "Mr. Smith and his wife have arranged to take their meals simultaneously" (YEAR-001-061).

- He patronizes the era's inventors, as when "Mr. Smith advanced $100,000 to the young chemist, and engaged his services for the Earth Chronicle laboratory" (YEAR-001-083).

## Appearances

- [[In the Year 2889]] — a single day in which his affairs span "transactions which involve a daily expenditure of upward of $800,000" (YEAR-001-113) and he travels by air, for "Smith stepped into his air-coach, which was in waiting for him at a window" (YEAR-001-067).

- His evening hosts a resurrection experiment, it "being the day set for his resurrection, it was proposed to Mr. Smith that he should permit the second part of the experiment to be performed at his residence this evening" (YEAR-001-088).
