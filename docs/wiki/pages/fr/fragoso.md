# Fragoso

## Overview

He is a rover of the great river — "Fragoso, in his capacity of wandering barber, had already run through the different provinces of South America" (EHLA-012-024).

Among the riverside tribes he is a figure of fame, "the celebrated Fragoso, barber in ordinary and extraordinary to the tribes of the Upper Amazon" (EHLA-012-061).

## Role in the Story

He owes his very life to the family, since the tale recalls "The deliverance of Fragoso at the time when he was dying of exhaustion in the forest of Iquitos" (EHLA-039-034).

When Joam is condemned he acts alone, for "Fragoso, without saying anything to anybody, secretly left the jangada" (EHLA-035-008) to seek the truth, and "Fragoso saw this, and pressed the chief of the band to tell him what he knew of this Ortega" (EHLA-038-024).

That name is the key, since "the name of Ortega, brought back by Fragoso, and which was the signature of the document" (EHLA-039-026) unlocks the cipher — after it was he who "undid the belt which encircled the body of Torres underneath the torn poncho" (EHLA-031-029) to recover the case.

## Character & Traits

He is quick and merry, sparkling "Fragoso with his witty repartees" (EHLA-018-046), and irrepressible, so that "Fragoso, in an exuberant moment, had tried to reply by a song in his own fashion" (EHLA-016-083).

Beneath the gaiety he is loyal — "Discreet and devoted, his services would have been most welcome to the two young fellows" (EHLA-036-005).

## Relationships

- Lina — the mulatto companion he loves and weds, for it was whispered that "the charming Lina was engaged to Fragoso" (EHLA-018-008).

- [[Minha Garral]] — her young mistress, who teases him gaily, "when you marry Lina, diamond takes the place of diamond, and you do not lose by the change" (EHLA-019-016).

- [[Manoel Valdez]] — his comrade in the family's ordeals, at whose side "Manoel and Fragoso, fifty paces further on, after swiftly running round one of the spurs of the bank" (EHLA-026-048) confront the villain.

- [[Torres]] — the blackmailer he half-remembers, being "Fragoso, who always had the idea that he had already met Torres somewhere" (EHLA-018-062).

## Appearances

- [[Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]] — where he dives to save Minha and "Fragoso reappeared on the surface of the Amazon--safe and sound" (EHLA-017-087).

- The voyage ends in a double wedding, "the marriage of Lina and Fragoso" (EHLA-040-034).
